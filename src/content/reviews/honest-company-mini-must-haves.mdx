The carry-on packing crisis hit at 11 PM the night before our first flight with an infant. We had the bottles packed, the diapers counted, the car seat gate-checked in our plan. Then we looked at the bath supplies. Full-size baby shampoo: 10 ounces — over the TSA limit. Body wash: 8 ounces — over the limit. Baby lotion: 12 ounces — not even close. We could check a bag, but our connecting flight had a 55-minute layover and we did not trust that a checked bag would make the transfer. We spent forty minutes pouring baby products into mismatched travel containers from a dollar store, labeling them with a Sharpie, and hoping TSA would not question the collection of unlabeled liquids we were about to present at security.

The Honest Company Mini Must Haves Gift Set exists to solve exactly that problem. Four travel-sized baby bath essentials — shampoo, body wash, face and body lotion, and bubble bath — all under TSA liquid limits, all in one compact package. At $10.97, the set costs more per ounce than buying full-size products and decanting them yourself. But the convenience of pre-portioned, clearly labeled, TSA-compliant baby bath products in a carry-on-ready set has made this our default purchase before every flight. We buy a new set for every trip longer than three nights. It is not the cheapest approach. It is the one that works at 11 PM the night before a 6 AM flight.

## Key Features Deep Dive

### TSA-Compliant Sizes

Every product in the set is under 3.4 fluid ounces — the TSA limit for carry-on liquids. The shampoo and body wash bottles are approximately 1-2 fluid ounces each. The lotion is similarly sized. All four fit comfortably inside a quart-size clear bag alongside a few other travel liquids. No decanting, no guessing, no Sharpie-labeled mystery bottles.

The TSA compliance is the entire value proposition of this set. You can buy full-size Honest Company products for better per-ounce value, but you cannot bring them in a carry-on. If you check luggage, the full-size bottles make more sense financially. If you carry on only — which is how we travel with a baby to avoid the risk of lost checked bags containing essential baby supplies — this set is the solution.

### Complete Bath Routine in One Package

The set includes shampoo, body wash, face and body lotion, and bubble bath. This covers the entire bedtime bath routine: wash hair, wash body, optional bubble bath for fun, moisturize after drying. Having the complete routine in travel sizes means you do not need to prioritize which products to bring and which to leave behind. Everything travels together.

The bubble bath inclusion is a thoughtful detail. Bubble bath is rarely an essential, but for a toddler in an unfamiliar hotel bathtub, bubbles transform a potentially anxious bath into a fun one. We have found that a small amount of bubble bath in a hotel tub changes our daughter's attitude from "suspicious of this strange bathtub" to "this is the best bathtub ever."

### Hypoallergenic Lavender Formulation

The products are hypoallergenic and use a gentle lavender scent. Lavender is widely associated with calming properties — whether the aromatherapy claim is clinically supported is debatable, but the practical reality is that a consistent lavender-scented bath routine signals "bedtime" to a baby. When that routine travels with you, the sensory cue travels too. Our daughter associates the lavender smell with bath-then-bed, and having that scent in a hotel room helps maintain the sleep routine in an unfamiliar environment.

## What We Don't Love

**The sizes run out fast.** Each product is 1-2 fluid ounces. With daily use, the shampoo and body wash last approximately four to five baths. For a three-night trip, this is adequate. For a week-long vacation, you will run out by day five. We buy two sets for trips longer than five nights, which doubles the cost.

**The per-ounce cost is steep.** The set costs $10.97 for approximately 5-6 combined fluid ounces of product. The full-size Honest Company shampoo is $9.97 for 10 fluid ounces. You are paying roughly three to four times the per-ounce price for the travel sizes. The premium is entirely for the convenience of pre-portioned TSA-compliant bottles.

**Lavender is not fragrance-free.** For babies with fragrance sensitivity, the lavender scent — however gentle — is still a fragrance. The Honest Company does not offer the Mini Must Haves set in a fragrance-free version. If your baby reacts to scented products, you need to decant the fragrance-free full-size versions into travel bottles instead.

**The packaging is not refillable.** The small bottles are single-trip products. You cannot easily refill them from full-size bottles — the openings are small and the bottles are not designed for repeated use. After each trip, the empty bottles go in recycling. For environmentally conscious families, the waste from buying a new set every trip is a legitimate concern.

## Real-World Testing

**Flights (9 trips):** Packed the set in the TSA quart bag on every flight. Zero security issues. The set fits alongside a tube of diaper cream and a small hand sanitizer in a standard quart bag. Total bath-related packing time: under thirty seconds.

**Hotel bath time (9 stays):** Used the complete set — shampoo, body wash, bubble bath, lotion — in hotel bathtubs ranging from cramped motel tubs to oversized resort tubs. The products performed identically to the full-size versions. The bubble bath produced moderate bubbles in all water types.

**3-night trips (5 trips):** One set lasted the entire stay with product remaining. The shampoo ran out first, followed by the body wash. The lotion and bubble bath lasted through all three nights. One set is the correct quantity for trips of three nights or fewer.

**7-night trip (2 trips):** One set ran out on night four. We supplemented with hotel-provided baby wash for the remaining nights, which was adequate but not ideal. For week-long trips, two sets are necessary. At $22 for two sets, the cost is noticeable but not prohibitive.

## How It Compares

**vs. Decanting full-size products ($3-5 for travel bottles):** Reusable travel bottles from a drugstore cost $3-5 and can be refilled indefinitely. This approach is cheaper long-term and produces less waste. The downside: you spend time filling, labeling, and managing multiple small bottles before every trip. For frequent travelers willing to maintain a travel bottle system, decanting is the better financial choice. For everyone else, the Mini Must Haves set is easier.

**vs. Cetaphil Baby Wash in a travel bottle ($6 + $2 bottle):** The Cetaphil is cheaper and fragrance-free, but it is only a body wash and shampoo — no lotion, no bubble bath. The Honest Company set provides the complete routine. For families who only need a cleanser, Cetaphil in a reusable travel bottle wins on price. For families who want the full routine, the set wins on completeness.

**vs. Johnson's Baby Travel Kit ($7):** Johnson's offers a similar travel set at a lower price. The ingredient lists differ — the Honest Company uses more naturally derived ingredients and avoids certain synthetic compounds. For parents who prioritize ingredient quality, the Honest Company justifies the premium. For parents who prioritize cost, the Johnson's set is functional and affordable.

For external use only. The bubble bath should be used in moderate amounts — excessive bubble bath can irritate sensitive skin or cause urinary tract irritation in girls. Store travel-size bottles upright to prevent cap leaks. Check expiration dates, particularly if purchasing gift sets that may have been in inventory for extended periods.
