## Key Features Deep Dive

### Snap-In Spoon Design

The soft-tip spoon stores in a molded slot on the underside of the lid. It snaps into place with an audible click and stays there through bag jostling, car rides, and being tossed around a diaper bag. To use: flip the lid over, unsnap the spoon, feed the baby. To store: snap the spoon back in, close the lid. The spoon and bowl are always together — a paired system that eliminates the "I have food but no spoon" scenario.

The spoon itself is soft-tipped and appropriately sized for a baby's mouth. It is not the best spoon we own — our favorite feeding spoon at home is a different brand — but it is perfectly adequate for travel. The soft tip is gentle on emerging teeth and sensitive gums.

### Sealed Lid

The lid snaps onto the bowl with a friction fit that holds contents inside under normal conditions. You can toss the sealed bowl into a diaper bag with reasonable confidence that pureed sweet potato will not coat the interior of the bag. We have transported filled bowls in upright and sideways orientations without leakage.

The seal is not airtight and not pressurized. If you squeeze the bowl firmly, the lid can pop off. If you drop the bowl lid-down, the impact can break the seal. Under normal handling — sitting in a bag, being carried in a hand — the seal holds. We fill it at home, transport it to the park, and open it at feeding time with the contents intact.

### BPA-Free and Dishwasher Safe

The bowl, lid, and spoon are BPA-free plastic. All three components go in the top rack of the dishwasher without warping, discoloring, or degrading. After eight months of daily dishwasher cycles, our original bowl still looks and functions like new.

The plastic is lightweight and shatter-resistant — critical for a product that will be thrown from a high chair, knocked off a table, and dropped on restaurant floors by a baby who finds gravity endlessly amusing.

## What We Don't Love

**The lid pops off under pressure.** If the bowl gets squeezed in a packed diaper bag — sandwiched between a wipes pack and a bottle — the lid can pop off. We learned this when pureed peas coated the inside of the diaper bag on a Tuesday morning. Now we position the bowl in the bag where it will not be compressed, and we have not had a repeat incident.

**The spoon is basic.** The included spoon works for feeding a baby, but it lacks the ergonomic grip, the depth, and the balance of our preferred home feeding spoon. For travel, "basic and present" beats "premium and forgotten." But if you have a spoon preference, the Dr. Brown's spoon may not match your home spoon's quality.

**8 ounces is one serving.** For a single park feeding, 8 ounces is perfect. For a day trip with multiple meals, you need to refill or bring multiple bowls. The small size that makes it portable also limits it to one meal per fill.

**No insulation.** The bowl is room-temperature plastic. Warm food cools quickly. Cold food warms quickly. If you prepare warm oatmeal at home and transport it to the park, it will be lukewarm by the time you feed. This is not a thermal container — plan for room-temperature meals when transporting for more than 15 minutes.
