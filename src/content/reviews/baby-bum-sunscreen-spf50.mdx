## Key Features Deep Dive

### Mineral (Zinc Oxide) Formula

The active ingredient is zinc oxide, a mineral that sits on the skin's surface and physically reflects UV rays. This is different from chemical sunscreens (oxybenzone, avobenzone, etc.) that absorb into the skin and neutralize UV rays chemically. The mineral approach is preferred by many pediatricians for babies because the ingredients stay on top of the skin rather than being absorbed.

Zinc oxide provides broad-spectrum protection — it blocks both UVA (aging, deep skin damage) and UVB (burning) rays. SPF 50 means it blocks approximately 98 percent of UVB rays when applied correctly. The protection begins immediately upon application (chemical sunscreens require 15–20 minutes to become effective).

### Fragrance-Free

Baby Bum SPF 50 has no added fragrance. This matters because fragrances are a common cause of skin irritation and allergic reactions in babies and young children. Many "baby" sunscreens include light scents that can trigger reactions on sensitive skin. The Baby Bum smells like nothing — or more precisely, it smells faintly like zinc oxide, which is a mild, inoffensive scent.

Our daughter has eczema-prone skin, and fragrance in skincare products triggers flare-ups. Baby Bum is one of the few sunscreens that has never caused a reaction. We have used it continuously for over a year across beach vacations, pool days, and outdoor play without any skin irritation.

### Travel Size (3 oz, TSA-Friendly)

The 3-ounce tube is under the TSA liquid limit, which means it goes in your quart bag without question. No need to check luggage just to bring sunscreen. No need to buy sunscreen at the destination at inflated resort prices.

Three ounces sounds small, but for a toddler, it covers more applications than you might expect. A single full-body application for a 2-year-old uses roughly 0.5–0.75 ounces. That means 4–6 full-body applications per tube — enough for a long weekend with reapplication, or a week-long trip with targeted application (exposed skin only when the child is dressed).

### Hawaii Act 104 Compliant (Reef-Safe)

The formula does not contain oxybenzone or octinoxate — two chemical sunscreen ingredients that research has linked to coral reef damage. Hawaii, Key West, Palau, and parts of the US Virgin Islands have banned sunscreens containing these chemicals. Baby Bum's mineral formula complies with all current reef-safe regulations.

For families traveling to tropical destinations, reef-safe sunscreen is not optional — it is legally required in some locations and environmentally responsible in all of them.

## What We Don't Love

**The white cast is real.** Mineral sunscreens leave a white tint because zinc oxide is a white mineral. On lighter skin, the cast fades to a mild sheen after rubbing in. On medium and darker skin tones, the white cast is noticeably visible and requires significant rubbing to minimize. Baby Bum is better than some mineral sunscreens in this regard, but the cast is still present.

**The formula is thick.** Baby Bum SPF 50 is a thick lotion that requires effort to spread evenly. It does not glide on like a chemical sunscreen. Applying it to a squirming toddler — who does not want to stand still for sunscreen application under any circumstances — takes patience and determination. We apply in sections: arms, then legs, then face, then back. Each section requires 10–15 seconds of rubbing.

**3 ounces runs out on long trips.** For a week-long beach vacation with daily full-body application and reapplication, one tube is not enough. We bring two tubes or supplement with a full-size tube in checked luggage. The travel size is perfect for weekend trips and carry-on-only travel; longer trips need more.

**It can sting if it gets in eyes.** Despite being a gentle mineral formula, zinc oxide sunscreen near the eyes can cause stinging if it runs with sweat. We apply less around the eye area and use a hat for face protection instead. This is common to virtually all sunscreens, not specific to Baby Bum.
