## Key Features Deep Dive

### Real Crib Construction

The Delta Children Mini Crib is built like a crib because it is one. The frame is solid wood with standard slatted sides that meet [CPSC crib safety](https://www.cpsc.gov/Safety-Education/Safety-Guides/Kids-and-Babies/Cribs) spacing requirements. The locking mechanisms are metal hardware, not plastic clips. When assembled, it feels as sturdy as the full-size crib in our nursery at home.

The interior dimensions are mini crib standard — about 38 by 24 inches. This is smaller than a full-size crib but larger than most playards. Our daughter had room to move comfortably from birth through 22 months, when she was approaching the height limit.

The construction means this crib will not sway, flex, or wobble when a toddler pulls themselves up on the rails. Playards, even good ones, have some inherent flex in their mesh and fabric construction. The Delta Mini Crib has none. Your baby leans on the rail, the rail does not move.

### GREENGUARD Gold Certification

GREENGUARD Gold is a third-party certification for low chemical emissions. The crib has been tested to confirm that it does not release harmful levels of volatile organic compounds (VOCs) into the room air. This matters because babies spend twelve to sixteen hours per day in their sleep environment, breathing whatever the furniture off-gasses.

Most playards and travel cribs do not carry this certification. The Delta Children Mini Crib is one of the few portable sleep options that does. For parents who choose organic mattresses and low-VOC paint for their home nursery, this crib extends that standard to travel.

### 1.5-Inch Mattress

The included mattress is 1.5 inches thick — thicker than any playard pad and firm enough to meet safe sleep standards. It is a real mattress, not a fold-out pad. The fitted sheet (sold separately) stays on properly because the mattress has defined edges rather than the rounded corners of a playard pad.

The mattress makes a noticeable difference in sleep quality. Our daughter's transition from home crib to this mini crib was seamless — no adjustment period, no fighting bedtime, no middle-of-the-night wake-ups from discomfort. The same child who refused to settle in a Pack 'N Play fell asleep in the mini crib within ten minutes on her first night.

### Folding Mechanism

The crib folds roughly in half along its length. The sides collapse inward and the mattress stays attached. The folded dimensions are approximately 38 by 25 by 6 inches — flat enough to slide behind a door, into a closet, or under a bed. This is remarkably compact for a piece of wooden furniture.

The fold and unfold process takes about two minutes in each direction. You lift the mattress platform, release two locking pins, fold the sides in, and lower the mattress back down. Unfolding is the reverse. The first time requires reading the instructions. After that, it becomes intuitive.

## What We Don't Love

**25 pounds is heavy.** Carrying the folded crib from the closet to the guest room is a two-hand job. Moving it between rooms or up stairs requires effort. This is not a crib you casually relocate — you set it up in one spot and leave it there for the duration of your visit.

**Not practical for air travel.** The weight and rigid folded dimensions make this a non-starter for flying. You cannot gate-check a wooden crib. For families who fly to visit grandparents, a playard or lightweight travel crib is the only option.

**The 24-month age limit comes earlier than full-size cribs.** Because of the mini dimensions, taller toddlers may outgrow the crib before age two. Our daughter was comfortable until 22 months at the 50th percentile for height. Taller children might outgrow it sooner.

**Sheets must be purchased separately.** The crib does not include a fitted sheet, and mini crib sheets are less commonly stocked in stores than standard crib sheets. Order them online ahead of time. Standard crib sheets will not fit — they are too large and create a suffocation risk.
